About Makeki Bakery

Our story, our team, our values.

How It Started

From a Kitchen in Kasarani

Makeki Bakery was born in 2018 when Wanjiku Mwangi, a former hotel pastry chef who decided to bring sourdough bread to Kasarani, Nairobi. Armed with a 10-litre Dutch oven and a sourdough starter she has fed for six years, she began selling loaves to neighbours every Saturday morning.

By 2020, Makeki had moved to a shopfront on Kariobangi Street in the Nairobi CBD, serving over 200 customers daily and supplying hotels across Nairobi.

The name "Makeki" is Swahili for cake; a reminder that every item we bake is made with celebration in mind.

What We Stand For

Our Values

Made with Love

Every loaf shaped by hand. No shortcuts, no industrial mixers. Just flour, water, salt, time, and care.

Local and Sustainable

Wheat from Eldoret farms. Dairy from Kiambu County cooperatives. Local first, always.

Community First

We donate 20 loaves every Friday to the St. John's Community Centre in Pumwani. Feeding Nairobi is a mission.
